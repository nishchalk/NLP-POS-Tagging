# NLP-POS-Tagging

PROBLEM STATEMENT - 3: Handling OOV Words in HMMs
Title: Unknown Word Handling and Smoothing in POS Tagging

Assigned Domains (Choose your group's assigned domain):

Domain: Gaming Forums / Discord Transcripts (Group - 125 & 126)

Tasks:

Load a dataset fitting your domain, parse the first 10,000 sentences, tag them, and build a frequency distribution of the words. (1 Mark)

Implement Laplace (Add-1) smoothing on your emission probabilities to ensure that no valid word-tag combination has a probability of zero. (2 Marks)

Design a morphological fallback function for Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) words. Apply at least 4 common English morphological rules (e.g., words ending in '-ing' are likely VBG, words ending in '-ly' are likely adverbs). (3 Marks)

Create a custom test sentence containing at least 3 completely fabricated/unseen domain-specific slang words. Use your model to tag the sentence and display the output. (2 Marks)

Discuss the limitations of standard HMMs when dealing with morphologically rich text or noisy domain data. How do morphological fallbacks improve model robustness? (2 Marks)
